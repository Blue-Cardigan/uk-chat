## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are people exploring UK civic and policy questions through chat, with answers grounded in live tools, maps, and visualizations. They need fast signal, trustworthy presentation, and clear visual hierarchy while switching between conversation and data panels.

### Brand Personality
Sleek, authoritative, modern.

The interface should feel precise and editorial rather than playful. It should communicate confidence and clarity for serious UK-focused analysis work.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a blue-red civic palette with cool blue-tinted neutrals:
- Blue is the primary interaction color for CTAs, active states, and focus.
- Red is an intentional accent color for highlights and contrast.
- Neutrals are cool and slightly blue-tinted in both light and dark themes.

Avoid warm brown themes, muddy neutrals, and generic AI-looking purple/cyan dark mode styling.

### Design Principles
1. Keep structure calm and readable: clear spacing, strong typographic hierarchy, and minimal visual clutter.
2. Reserve saturated color for meaning: accents should be purposeful, not decorative.
3. Maintain token consistency: all surfaces and states should derive from shared semantic color tokens.
4. Ensure dark mode quality is equal to light mode, not an inverted afterthought.
5. Prioritize legibility and accessibility in every panel, chart, and control.
